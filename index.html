The Centre for Innovative Education and Development Research in India celebrates World Children’s Day every year 20th November, International Children’s Day is a day dedicated to celebrating and promoting the well-being and rights of children around the world. It is a day to recognize and endorse the importance of children in society, highlighting their potential, aspirations, and the need to create a safe and nurturing environment for them to thrive. This lively event features games, educational activities, and inspiring speeches, fostering a joyful and inclusive environment for kids to learn and grow.

EVENT THEME (SUGGESTIONS)
  1. Indian Folk Tales and Mythology: Encourage children to dress up as characters from popular Indian folk tales or mythological stories, such as Ramayana, Mahabharata, or stories from regional folklore.
  1. Historical Figures: Let kids portray famous historical figures from India’s rich history, like Mahatma Gandhi, Jawaharlal Nehru, Rani Lakshmi Bai, or other prominent personalities.
  2. Wildlife and Nature: Children can dress up as animals, birds, or elements of nature found in India, promoting environmental awareness and conservation.
  3. States of India: Each child can represent a different Indian state, showcasing its unique culture, traditions, and famous personalities.
  4. Famous Indian Personalities: Kids can choose to dress as their favorite contemporary Indian personalities, be it sports stars, actors, or scientists.
  5. Superheroes with an Indian Twist: Combine the concept of superheroes with Indian elements to create unique characters. For example, “Indian Superheroes” with special powers related to Indian culture.
  6. Cultural Festivals: Children can represent various Indian festivals like Diwali, Holi, Eid, Christmas, or any regional festivals, highlighting the cultural diversity of India.
  7. National Symbols: Let kids become the living symbols of India, such as the national flag, peacock, tiger, or any other national symbol.
  8. Space and Science: Encourage children to explore the world of space and science by dressing up as astronauts, scientists, or celestial bodies.
  9. Historical Eras: Kids can transport themselves to different historical eras of India, like the Mughal Empire, the Indus Valley Civilization, or the British Raj.
  10. Remember to choose a theme that is suitable for the age group of the children participating and provides them with an opportunity to learn, be creative, and have fun.
